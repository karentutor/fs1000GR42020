South Africa

Unique Facts about South Africa

  • Reflecting the country's diversity, South Africa has 11 official languages
  • South Africa has three capital cities: Pretoria hosts the executive branch, Cape Town is home to the legislative branch and Bloemfontein is home to the judicial branch.
  • South Africa is the only country to voluntarily abandon its nuclear weapons program.
  • South Africa is extremely rich in mining and minerals and considered the world’s leader with nearly 90% of all the platinum metals on earth and around 41% of all the world’s Gold

Egypt

Unique Facts about Egypt

  • The Egyptians invented the 365-days a year calendar
  • Greater Cairo is the largest city in Africa and the Middle East
  • Egypt is home to seven UNESCO-designated World Heritage sites .
  • The Eastern Desert comprises almost one-fourth of the land surface of Egypt and covers an area of about 85,690 square miles (221,900 square km).
Source- Britannica.com
Nigeria

Unique Facts about Nigeria

  • The town of Igbo-Ora is known as the nation's home of twins. Many of the local Yoruba people believe their consumption of yams and okra leaves to be the cause of their high birth rate of twins
  • Nigeria is a diverse multiethnic country with more than 520 spoken languages. While English is the official language, Hausa, Yoruba and Igbo are also major languages in the country.
  • The country's film industry, known as Nollywood, is one of the largest film producers in the world, second only to India's Bollywood.
  • Largely due to its export market, Nigeria is the largest economy in Africa.
Source- usnews.com
Kenya

Unique Facts about Kenya

  • In Kenya, more than 60 languages are spoken and there are more than 40 ethnic groups. Almost everyone there speaks more than one African language.
  • Millions of people visit Kenya each year to see its endless savannah and the animals that inhabit it: elephants, lions, cheetahs, giraffes, zebras, hippos, rhinos and more. The Kenyan government has set up more than 50 reserves and parks to protect these animals
  • Kenya’s Kalenjin tribe has been dubbed ‘the running tribe’ for its unbelievable ability to spawn world record breaking runners.
  • Mount Kenya is the highest mountain in Kenya and the second-highest (after Mt Kilimanjaro) in Africa.
Source- National Geographic.com
